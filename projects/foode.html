WHAT'S FOODE

Food-e is an entrepreneur project based in University of Michigan. It is a communication platform that connects restaurant owners and customers with honest and private feedbacks.

Customers leave anonymous suggestions that are only visible to restaurant owners

After user visit a restaurant, they can choose to leave a subjective suggestion or take a quick survey to help the restaurant improve. These suggestions and survey results are only visible to restaurant owners.

Scope:
Build customer-end mobile app from scratch

Out of Scope:
Build restaurant-end App

The product owner (our client) conducted sufficient market research with partner restaurants beforehand, and had clear product vision for the restaurant-end app with basic functions of content creation, survey analysis etc.

However, he didn’t get to research and validate the true motivations for customers to get involved and leave feedbacks. Therefore, we reached an agreement on the scope for this project, which was simply focusing on researching and developing the customer-end App.

ROAD TO MINIMUM VIABLE PRODUCT

Identify potential users by two types of user research

In-Restaurant Observation & Interview

  • We created a restaurant feedback-gathering typeform (the traditional method that a lot of restaurants are using).
  • We observed how 6 users interact with web forms in our partner restaurant and interviewed to understand their motivations, frustrations and concerns.

Offsite Interview & Concept Testing

  • After in-restaurant user research, we gathered preliminary information on user persona, and brainstormed 6 ideas accordingly.
  • We conducted 6 more user interviewsto discuss these 6 ideas respectively and drew insights for low-fi prototyps.

So, what kind of customers are more likely to use Food-e?

Shy to confront in-person

Customers are shy to give in-person feedback. They feel pressured to do so, and afraid to appear as "rude".

Don't want to go out of their way

If giving feedback is easy and quick, most customers are willing to do it.

Willing to return with coupons

80% of interviewees expressed willingness to return to restaurant if given coupons/perks.

Willing to return if feel involved

56% of interviewees expressed willingness to return to restaurant if they know restaurant make changes based on their feedback, because this makes them feel "cool", "helpful", and "proud".

TESTING

Identify design opportunities with testing & Bye bye my graduate student years

So we had our MVP, what next? It was a great chance for me to conduct thorough usability testings with post-testing interviews.

Along with a big list of usability problems, I discovered design opportunities as well. The MVP is essentially just a list of restaurants, and it barely attracts potential users as intended. Answering these “How Might We”s might be hard, but it would help Food-e meets its value proposition.

  • Challenge 1: (Create more value for restaurants)
    How might we incentivize user to leave quality feedbacks?
  • Challenge 2: (Create loyal users for both restaurants and Food-e)
    How might we increase long-term user engagemnt?

CHALLENGE 1: INCENTIVIZE QUALITY FEEDBACK

How might we incentivize user to leave quality feedbacks?

/User tends to finish fast with low quality
During user testing, we noticed that user tends to skip text fields at the end, which prevent the restaurants from getting quality, detailed feedbacks. Therefore, my design focused on nudging customers to put in more contents with improved usability.

/User is lack of internal drive
Another finding is that users are currently driven by external motivations, coupons. To create internal motivations of “helping the restaurant”, my design focused on highlighting that they are helping local business. Also, this is related to Challenge 3.

Improvement 1: Diversify incentives for different types of users

I split the current “review” feature into two different features for two types of users. User can behave differently based on their thoughts on specific restaurants, and use these two features differently.

Improvement 2: Improve core experiences for survey and suggestion

MVP testing revealed a lot of usability issues in existing UI. When dividing review into “Survey” and “Suggestion”, I improved the core experience with these insights.

The main focus is to show user status and highlight primary actions with clear visual cues.

CHALLENGE 2: GAIN LONG TERM ENGAGEMENT

How might we increase long-term user engagemnt?

In the process of developing MVP, we tested out multiple engagement methods including badge system and level system.

However, after the final usability testing, we reached the conclusion that such social-focused incentives have small impacts on motivating our users because tall the reviews are private (visible only to user and the restaurant owner).

In order to create a more dynamic and long-lasting Food-e community that drives user to constantly leave new suggestions and pursue more than just coupons, I designed for two major improvements.

Improvement 1: Create constant engagement with invitation feature

Improvement 2: Make user feel more involved by communication features

In the post-testing interview of MVP, users expressed that they don't know if their suggestion are adopted or not. Therefore, new design focuses on creating a mechanism that restaurant and user can communicate. Also, another insight came up that user might not use the coupon right after they receive it, the new design proposes two different flows for claiming coupon upon confirmation (under Profile Tab), and actually redeem coupon in store (under Wallet Tab).
